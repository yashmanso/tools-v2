<h1>Overview</h1>
<p>The Sustainable Value Analysis Tool is designed to aid manufacturers in recognizing and capitalizing on sustainable value creation opportunities throughout a product's life cycle. By analyzing captured and uncaptured value from the beginning of life (BoL), through the middle of life (MoL), to the end of life (EoL), the tool enables companies to systematically identify areas where value is missed, destroyed, surplus, or absent. It consists of a poster for visual mapping and a set of cards for guidance, combining life cycle thinking with value forms analysis. The five-step process involves defining life cycle stages, mapping stakeholder value, identifying uncaptured value, pinpointing value opportunities, and assessing their feasibility and sustainability. While the tool is comprehensive and strategically generates business opportunities, it may lack the necessary guidance on strategic planning for realizing these opportunities.</p>
<hr>
<h1>Dimensions</h1>
<p><strong>Resource Type</strong>
This tool provides a structured approach and visual aids to help companies identify and leverage uncaptured value.</p>
<ul>
<li>Tags: #tool, #framework, #method</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Objective</strong>
The primary goal of this resource is to map out and assess the captured and uncaptured value throughout the product life cycle to make sure that the organization is aligned with sustainable practices.</p>
<ul>
<li>Tags: #map, #assess, #align</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Target Audience</strong>
The tool is designed for stakeholders in the manufacturing sector, including entrepreneurs and companies focused on sustainability.</p>
<ul>
<li>Tags: #entrepreneurs, #practitioners, #corporations, #manufacturing</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Sustainability Focus</strong>
It addresses all three pillars of sustainability by considering environmental, social, and economic dimensions in value analysis at the same time and in relation to each other.</p>
<ul>
<li>Tags: #environmental-sustainability, #social-sustainability, #economic-sustainability</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Innovation Type</strong>
The tool supports innovation in product life cycles and business models by identifying new opportunities for sustainable value creation. The tool itself is mostly designed to assess the sustainability relevance thoroughout the life cycle.</p>
<ul>
<li>Tags: #product-innovation, #business-model-innovation</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Entrepreneurship Stage</strong>
This resource is applicable across various stages of entrepreneurship, particularly in the design, development, and implementation phases where sustainable value creation is crucial. Similar to other tools, the sooner the assessment is done, the better an organization can react to align within principles of sustainability.</p>
<ul>
<li>Tags: #design, #development, #implementation</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Scope of Relevance</strong>
There is no explicit reference to the scope, but the focus on the manufacturing sector signals its relevance both to local as well as global contexts.</p>
<ul>
<li>Tags: #global, #local</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Methodological Approach</strong>
The tool employs a systematic, life cycle-based approach to value analysis, which is both qualitative and strategic in nature.</p>
<ul>
<li>Tags: #qualitative-research, #theoretical-frameworks</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Skill Development</strong>
Users of this tool will enhance their skills in systems thinking, environmental awareness, and innovative thinking for sustainable entrepreneurship.</p>
<ul>
<li>Tags: #systems-thinking, #environmental-awareness, #innovative-thinking, #resource-management</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Source and Credibility</strong>
The tool is based on academic research and by researchers at the University of Cambridge and is based in the foundations of sustaunable development goals (SDGs).</p>
<ul>
<li>Tags: #academic-institutions, #recognized-experts</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Prerequisites and Requirements</strong>
The tool is designed for users with at least an intermediate level of understanding of sustainability and life cycle analysis in a business context.</p>
<ul>
<li>Tags: #intermediate-level, #specific-educational-background</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Collaboration Level</strong>
The tool encourages collaboration among different stakeholders involved in the product life cycle, from design to disposal.</p>
<ul>
<li>Tags: #team, #cross-sector-partnerships</li>
</ul>
